Da'Mon Smith

Da'Mon Stith is an African American martial artist, researcher, and innovator. He was born in Austin, Texas in 1975 and became passionate about martial arts at the age of six after his father gave him a toy sword. Stith has taught at local schools, community centers, daycares, and fitness centers for 25 years. He is the founder of Guild of the Silent Sword and the president of the Historical African Martial Arts Association (HAMA).

Ashley White

an award-winning director, fight & intimacy director, and teaching artist, with international credits and nearly two decades of experience. As a director, she has helmed many productions, each of which incorporate significant play and pursuit of creative risk.

Tobie Minor

Toby has been teaching Stage Combat at Texas State University since 2012. He is an award-winning actor and fight choreographer all around Austin.

David Saldivar

David Saldivar is a certified Advanced Actor Combatant with the Society of American Fight Directors. He has been practicing martial arts since his early teens and studying stage combat since 2012. He graduated from University of Texas at Arlington in 2015 with a Bachelors in Theatre Arts. Recently, he has been studying Bolognese Sidesword with Dallas Renaissance Sword Guild in the Dallas area.
